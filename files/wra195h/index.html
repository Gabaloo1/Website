Wait, What?

It all started with a “Wait, what?”. Already in college, it struck me to realize I couldn’t figure how would I belong to my field without knowing it, without even being at MSU! Fortunately, WRA 195H helped me explore, immerse, and deepen in the area I desire to pursue: Computer Science. Here’s how.

How something I love helps me suceed at MSU?

To belong, I first needed to identify what I already possess. I realized that even the most mundane hobbies or passions could contribute to my scholarly success in unimaginable ways.
Skating? Drawing? Equitation? I learned from my peers how each of these activities impacted their life and, most importantly, how did they contribute to their scholarly success and boosted their literacy skills (reading, writing, researching).

I decided to explore my passionate interest in languages. As a non-English speaker, I've come to acknowledge the value and importance of the world's tongues. I speak Spanish and adventured with Mandarin some years ago. I consider myself an amateur in Japanese, French, and Quechua.
I ended up learning that, indeed, my interest in languages profoundly benefitted the way I saw the world through reading, changed the essence of my writing, and enriched my pursue of researching.

Now... into exploring the literacy of Computer Science

Synthesizing my findings

In order to encapsulate and display what I had learned about my field, I elaborated a Research Essay in which I carefully explored a single branch of the field (and probably my favorite): Artificial Intelligence.

I examined diverse sources of scholarly journal articles and trade publications to figure how knowledge is communicated, created, and preserved in the field through the use of literacy skills.

Likewise, I investigated people working in the field and interviewed some of them in order to learn about their motivations and the life issues they go through.

This SWAP analysis summarizes my essay:

How is knowledge built and communicated in the field of Artificial Intelligence, and what quality of life issues affect people involved in it.

Student pursuing a Computer Science degree focused on research and interested in the field of Artificial Intelligence.

Undergraduate and graduate students interested in specializing in the field of Artificial Intelligence, especially in the research area. Investigators conducting interdisciplinary research related to Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence.

Show a deeper insight into how the knowledge used to develop technological advancements with Artificial Intelligence applications is based on the foundations of reading, writing, and research as part of scholar and professional success; and how do people working in the field use these tools in their day-to-day.

After performing the research, I concluded that investigation in AI mainly aims to (1) improve the application of a certain feature or (2) create new features for known or unkown applications.

AI research is directed to enhancing and developing new algorithms or applications of features that could then be useful in the technological and commercial areas. Therefore, we could say that knowledge in the field is developed and created upon existent knowledge, AI is a growing and permanently evolving field, thus existent practices, even those considered state-of-the-art, are prone to be improved.

In order to conduct such a specific type of investigation, academics in the AI field must involve in more narrowed-down secondary research. Throughout the articles analyzed, the authors include several references to previous models related by extracting useful features to rescue and flaws to improve.

Ultimately, these research articles are published in journals of the AI field so they are shared in the academia and can be eventually applied to the real-world.

Remix Project

Throughout this journey, I developed more comfort and a stronger sense of belonging to my field with each step I took.

The next action was to communicate its importance to others and share the passion and excitement I feel for the field. Either to motivate them to follow a career or just share some interdisciplinary advice on scholarly success.

Along with Juan, Joanna, Matthew, and Chandan, we designed an engaging presentation in which we gathered our findings of Computer Science and displayed them to everyone aiming to spark interest in our area.

Finally, I wanted to shared this with everyone out there. That's why I decided to build and release this webpage, so any high school student or exploring undergrad can be engaged by this story: the journey of finding belonging to Computer Science through WRA 195H.
